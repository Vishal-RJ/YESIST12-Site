About YESIST12

Youth Endeavours For Social Innovation Using Sustainable Technology

Youth Endeavors for Social Innovation using Sustainable Technology is a global platform that encourages students, school children, and young professionals to develop innovative tech solutions for real-world social challenges. With tracks like the Innovation Challenge, Junior Einstein, and WePOWER, it promotes creativity, sustainability, and inclusion. Participants receive expert mentorship, international exposure, and a chance to present their ideas on a global stage. YESIST12 empowers youth with hands-on experience and essential skills, acting as a launchpad for socially driven innovation and impactful careers.

YESIST12 GATEWAYS

Pilot Entry
  • The Pilot Entry pathway allows students to take part in YESIST12 through a nearby or associated Pilot Organization officially recognized by IEEE YESIST12.
  • Participants need to submit their abstracts to these designated pilot centers, which will host a preliminary selection round or screening event.
  • The pilots will conduct the first level of scrutiny, evaluating submissions based on the given guidelines and judging criteria.
  • The best-performing teams from this pilot round will have their entries forwarded to the YESIST12 core committee for a final review and approval.
  • After this review, the confirmed teams will be notified about their qualification for the Grand Finale, along with further instructions.
Direct Entry
  • For participants who do not have a recognized pilot center nearby, the Direct Entry option allows them to showcase their ideas without geographical limitations.
  • These direct submissions will undergo a thorough screening and evaluation process by the central YESIST12 review panel, ensuring that every idea gets a fair and unbiased assessment.
  • Once the screening is complete, the selected teams will receive official communication from the YESIST12 organizing team regarding their selection and the further steps, including registration for the Grand Finale.
  • This pathway ensures that students from remote areas or regions without a local pilot center still have the opportunity to compete at the international level.
