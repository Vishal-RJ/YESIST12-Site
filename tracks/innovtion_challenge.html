About Innovation Track

In a world driven by creativity and problem-solving, the Innovation Challenge Track emerges as a platform for fostering groundbreaking ideas. This track inspires participants to think beyond conventional boundaries, align their innovative ideas with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), and showcase their vision for a sustainable future. By focusing on ideation without the requirement for physical prototypes, this track ensures inclusivity and broad participation, empowering changemakers from diverse backgrounds.

Key Focus Areas

  • Creative Solutions for Global Challenges: Innovative ideas addressing pressing issues such as climate change, poverty, education, and healthcare.
  • Scalability and Impact: Ideas that are scalable and capable of creating significant positive impact.
  • Inclusivity and Accessibility: Solutions that promote inclusivity, ensuring diverse communities benefit equally.
  • Technological Integration: Leveraging advancements in AI, IoT, renewable energy, and other cutting-edge technologies to address challenges.
  • Alignment with SDGs: Ideas that contribute to the realization of the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals.

Objectives:

The main objective of the Innovation Track in YESIST12 is to harness creative thinking and innovative solutions to address the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). It aims to achieve this by:

Encourage Ideation

Inspire participants to dream big and think creatively.

Promote Inclusivity

Provide an open platform that removes barriers to innovation and welcomes diverse participants.

Foster Problem-Solving

Encourage actionable and impactful solutions to global challenges.

Empower Changemakers

Showcase ideas with the potential to drive meaningful global change.

Who can Participate?

Participants in the Innovation Challenge Track 2025 are diverse and inclusive, welcoming individuals passionate about addressing global challenges through innovative ideas.

Team Details

  • Team Size : In the range of 1 to 4 members.
  • Team Registration : Teams must register with a chosen mentor through Pilot entry or Direct Entry.
  • Eligibility : Open to university students, innovators, and professionals dedicated to sustainable development.

Benefits

  • Enhance Employability : Gain recognition for innovative thinking, which boosts employability and career prospects.
  • Skill Development : Build practical problem-solving and creative thinking skills through project-based learning.
  • Networking Opportunities : Interact with industry experts, mentors, and peers in an international setting.
  • Recognition and Awards : Gain acknowledgment for impactful ideas at an international platform.
  • Post-Competition Opportunities : Access potential mentorship for project development and future entrepreneurial ventures.

What Projects can be submitted?

The Innovation Challenge Track welcomes projects that align with the theme “Dream Bigger, Solve Holistically, Impact Globally” and focus on the following areas:

  • AI and Digital Transformation : Leveraging AI and IoT to address global challenges.
  • Renewable Energy and Sustainability : Innovative solutions for sustainable energy and environmental conservation.
  • Healthcare and Education : Ideas enhancing accessibility and quality in healthcare and education.
  • Community-Driven Initiatives : Grassroots innovations addressing local challenges and fostering societal progress.
  • Technology for Inclusive Growth : Technological advancements that promote inclusivity and equity across communities.
  • Open Innovation : Projects that embrace collaborative, cross-disciplinary, and unconventional approaches to create groundbreaking solutions in any domain.

FAQs:

1. Who is eligible to participate?
Open to students, professionals, and innovators passionate about sustainable development and innovation.
2. What can be the strength of each team?
Teams can have 1 to 4 members.
3. Is it compulsory to have a mentor?
Yes, teams must register with a chosen mentor.
4. Can the project idea deviate from the theme?
While alignment with the theme is encouraged, projects with significant impact potential are welcome.
5. What are the submission guidelines?
Submissions must follow the IEEE YESIST12 branding template.
6. Can abstracts be revised post-submission?
Yes, revisions are allowed before the submission deadline.
7. How are projects evaluated?
Projects are judged based on creativity, relevance to SDGs, scalability, and deliverability.
